Dartmouth Outing Club Concept Platform

Summary: The Dartmouth Outing Club has been a cornerstone of Dartmouth since its inception in 1909. While the DOC outdoor resources are extensive, they are currently underused by students and faculty. We set out to create a platform that would integrate student’s experiences in the classroom with outdoor exploration.

Role: UX designer and user research lead (team size: 3)

Timeline: 2 weeks (May 2017)

Context: ENGS 12: Design Thinking class, final project

Description: Our platform allows any professor to book DOC cabins for experiential learning excursions, and provides the infrastructure for professors and students to interact and coordinate necessary trip details. Increasing the prevalence of academic immersion in the outdoors will address the white space that currently exists between the outdoors' potential for experiential learning and the amount of students using DOC resources.

Challenge: How might we make full use of Dartmouth’s manifold resources by engaging Dartmouth students in the outdoors?

User Research

We conducted several rounds of interviews with 40 students, 10 faculty members, and 2 administrators, including Brian Kunz, DOC director.. We gathered two main insights: 1) Students face barriers of entry to the DOC and therefore don’t get enough opportunities to engage with the outdoors at Dartmouth 2) Dartmouth and the DOC have amazing resources in terms of cabins and equipment. However, these resources are currently underused by both students and faculty.

Brainstorm

To materialize our vision, the solution had to be: Accessible: we wanted to make planning a trip easy so that spending time in the outdoors would be more accessible for any student Inclusive: open to all groups on campus Relaxing & creative: the outdoors provide a great environment for students to get away from the Dartmouth stress bubble and have room to be creative Community-oriented: we envisioned an experience that would provide students and faculty an opportunity to bond while exploring nature and learning in the outdoors.

We wanted to address the white space between the students who would benefit from the outdoors and the DOC resources. By providing an experiential learning opportunity, we brainstormed creating a platform that would reach a great number of students who would ideally have this experience two to three times in their Dartmouth career. This would create a positive feedback loop and encourage students to keep experiencing the outdoors on their own afterwards.

Solution

Our innovation was driven by our desire to make full use of these outdoor resources and open them up to the whole campus. In short, we wanted to enrich students’ experience and well-being by engaging in the Dartmouth outdoors. With these insights in mind, we envisioned a platform that would provide professors and students with the opportunity to easily organize immersive academic experiences in the outdoors. Our platform would provide all the information needed for any faculty member to effectively plan an outdoor trip for their students and expose them to a positive experience outside in nature.

User Journey

Web Platform

The platform would have a straightforward interface for professors to interact with. They would be able to choose a date, time and whether they want to plan an overnight or a day trip. Then, the available cabins during that timeframe would show on the screen. The professor would be able to filter the options using filters such as distance from campus and capacity. We decided to include the filter option based on our discussions with several professors. The information about each cabin has already been compiled on the DOC website, so we envision that our platform could potentially be incorporated with it in the future.

The organizer of the trip would be able to see the students in his class that are able to participate as well as list the activities that will be included in the trip. The trip page will also include a summary of the professor’s goals for the experiential learning adventure.

Lastly, the calendar feature on the right would showcase other trips that are going out at similar times and thus the academic immersion could become interdisciplinary.

“The only problem is you can’t make [these trips] mandatory, so you always have a few stragglers… I keep doing them because its worth it for those involved” – E.O. Earth Sciences

Reflection

Next Steps

1) Develop and design the actual web application: establish a partnership with DALI Lab

2) Faculty and student buy-in: promote the platform to professors and students and engage as many academic departments as possible

3) Build stronger relationships with the DOC trip leaders, who would help facilitate some of these trips in the future

4) Extend to other on-campus groups: in the future, we envision that our platform would cater to student groups on campus such as acapella groups, dance groups, the Greek system, the House system, etc. Ideally, the amazing Dartmouth outdoor resources would open up to any and all groups on campus

5) Expand beyond cabins: immersive learning in the outdoors could make use of other resources that the DOC has to offer

6) Interdisciplinary: different academic departments would be able to coordinate trips together; e.g. the film and theatre departments could spend an afternoon together working on a film short

7) tests are outdoors: by taking advantage on the mental health benefits that the outdoor provides, in the future our platform could help alleviate exam stress (i.e. oral exams could take place in a relaxing environment in a cabin)
